Thesis project: "Effects of Parity-Violating Gravitational Waves on the Cosmic Microwave Background"

Universidad de Chile, August 2025 - Present

Abstract: Inflation provides a well-motivated framework for the generation of parity-violating effects in the early Universe. The simplest inflationary models have two fields: the inflaton and graviton, and the leading-order parity-violating interaction between them is described by Chern-Simons (CS) gravity. This interaction induces a parity-violating signature in primordial tensor perturbations. Such perturbations correspond to primordial chiral gravitational waves (CGWs), as the two tensor helicities evolve differently. This chiral asymmetry is subsequently transferred to the polarization of photons through Thomson scattering and becomes imprinted on the cosmic microwave background (CMB). In this work, we will analyze the evolution of primordial chiral gravitational waves (CGWs) across horizon crossing during radiation- and matter-dominated eras, and investigate their role as sources of the cosmic microwave background (CMB) E- and B-mode polarization. We will compute the induced E-B cross-correlation and compare the resulting predictions with current observational constraints.

Introduction to Research Course: "Forecast validation for Euclid cosmological probes using Fisher matrix formalism"

Universidad de Chile, March 2024 - December 2024

The Fisher matrix formalism is a valuable tool for validating how data will be collected by Euclid. This matrix has been calculated based on various cosmological models and plays a crucial role in determining how well current or future experiments can constrain cosmological parameters. It is also used to define the confidence regions within which the data is validated.

Research internship: "Genetic Algorithms: Machine learning in aid of cosmology."

Universidad de Chile, January 2024

A Genetic Algorithm consists of an optimization and search technique inspired on the principles of the evolution via natural selection. It simulates the development of a population of possible solutions over several generations. The most appropiate solutions are more likely to survive and pass on their characteristics to subsequent generations. A genetic algorithm is utilized to estimate the Hubble parameter H(z) based on the obtained data. The objective is to find the solution that best fits this data.
